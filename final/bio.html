Biography

Born on September 3rd, 1991 in Adrian, MI, I spent the entirety of my childhood growing up in the small town of Onsted, MI just 15 minutes from Adrian in Lenawee County. From an early age I had an extensive vocabulary and was often praised for being extremely well behaved in school. My interest in computers and computer related systems began around the age of thirteen when I built my first desktop computer from scratch (with the help of my uncle in picking out compatible parts to ensure that my creation would actually work.)

During my High School career I was involved in many extracurricular activities and accumulated many awards. I was in the marching and concert bands as a percussionist from 6th grade until just after my Junior year of High School. I also played baseball and basketball all four years, as well as playing football my senior year. As a baseball player I earned the most improved player award as a pitcher and third baseman, described as a "bulldog" by coaches and teammates when describing my work ethic. As a student athlete I was awarded the scholar athlete award nine times (one for each semester I played a sport.)

I was also a member of the Onsted Academic Club and a member of the National Honors Society. After graduating High School, I was able to secure a summer internship at a nearby automotive stamping plant. While I started as an intern in the quality control department I soon got re-assigned to the IT department as I had originally been hoping to intern in IT.

Entering my Freshman year at MSU I struggled mightily to adjust, finishing Freshman year on final probation after going through an appeals process to prove I was dedicated to raising my GPA. The past two years I have raised my GPA back to 2.55.
